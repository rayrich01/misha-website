## THE CORE INSIGHT

Houston's ultra-luxury housing market in 2025 was concentrated in
River Oaks and Memorial. Buyers in this segment arrive with their
architects and designers already engaged, thinking about long-term
customization. They are not searching "cheap painter houston."

They are searching for a craftsman who has worked in their neighborhood,
knows its architectural character, and has a portfolio that proves it.

Misha has that. 25 years. The portfolio exists. The credentials exist.
The notable projects exist. What has not existed is content that speaks
directly to the person standing in their River Oaks entry hall wondering
what to do with the walls.

## PUBLICATIONS — WHERE MISHA'S CLIENTS READ

These are the publications that reach Houston's luxury homeowner and
design community. They are also the PR targets for editorial coverage.

### PRIMARY TARGETS

**PaperCity Magazine** (papercitymag.com)
- Houston's dominant luxury lifestyle publication since 1994
- Average household income: $235,000+
- 60%+ female audience — the design decision maker
- Covers: home design, real estate, society, luxury lifestyle
- Key section: Home Design / PaperCity Design Awards (annual April event)
- PR angle: Grand Marble Estate case study, Venetian plaster feature
- Contact approach: Pitch to home design editor with portfolio photography

**Houstonia Magazine** (houstoniamag.com)
- Authoritative Houston monthly — "Smart. Authoritative. Entertaining."
- Architecture & Design section covers residential projects
- Notable: Featured Venetian fireplace and custom finishes in home tour coverage
- 2025 trend coverage: immersive design, bold textures, statement surfaces
- PR angle: "Houston home tour" style feature on a completed River Oaks project
- Contact approach: Architecture & Design section editor

**CultureMap Houston** (houston.culturemap.com)
- Digital-first Houston lifestyle publication
- Home Design section actively covers renovation trends and local designers
- 2025 coverage: Houzz trend reports, Houston luxury real estate
- PR angle: Trend piece — "Why Houston designers are specifying Venetian plaster"
- Contact approach: Home & Design editor

**Modern Luxury Interiors Texas** (modernluxury.com/texas)
- Print + digital, quarterly
- Co-hosts Houston Design Center Sip & Stroll events
- Covers: luxury residential interiors, Houston designers, finished homes
- PR angle: Full project feature — before/after with designer partner
- Contact approach: Pitch as designer collaboration story

**Houston Chronicle** (houstonchronicle.com)
- Home design section covers local craftspeople and notable renovations
- Broader reach than luxury-only publications
- PR angle: "25 years transforming Houston's most distinguished homes"

### SECONDARY / TRADE TARGETS

**Houston Design Center** (thehoustondesigncenter.com)
- 17-acre campus at 7026 Old Katy Road — the trade hub of Houston design
- Showrooms for designers, builders, architects
- Quarterly Sip & Stroll events attended by Houston's design community
- Opportunity: Presence at Sip & Stroll, partnership with showrooms,
  possibly exhibiting portfolio work within the center

**ASID Texas Chapter** — American Society of Interior Designers
- Awards program (Ruby Award, Design Awards)
- Networking events attended by Houston's working designers
- Opportunity: Submit projects for awards consideration with designer partners

## PR STRATEGY — GETTING INTO THE PUBLICATIONS

**The pitch for PaperCity Design Awards:**
- Entry category: Decorative Finishes / Artisan Craft
- Supporting material: Grand Marble Estate or River Oaks project photography
- Designer partner: Submit jointly with the designer who specified the work
- Timeline: April annual event — pitch in January

**The Houstonia pitch:**
- Home tour / architecture feature on a completed project
- Angle: "A River Oaks estate transformed by Houston's most celebrated
  decorative artist" — focus on the craft story
- Requires: Professional photography of the completed space
  (this is the single most important investment)

**The Houston Design Center opportunity:**
- Approach: Contact the Center about exhibiting portfolio photography
  in the public gallery or common areas
- Attend the quarterly Sip & Stroll with business cards and a QR code
  linking to studio.mishacreations.com
- This is direct access to every Houston designer and architect
  who sources from the Center
